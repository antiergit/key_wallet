Key Wallet Privacy Policy


  1. Purpose


    This Privacy Policy explains how Key Wallet ("we," "our," or "us") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our services. By accessing or using Key Wallet, you confirm that you are at least 18 years of age and consent to the practices described in this policy. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring your personal data is handled responsibly and securely. We also ensure compliance with major privacy regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).


  2. Privacy Policy


    1. Introduction


      The purpose of this Privacy Policy is to outline how Key Wallet handles and protects user data, emphasizing our commitment to your privacy and security. We aim to provide transparency about our data practices and empower users with knowledge about their rights and choices. Key Wallet operates globally, and we adhere to all applicable privacy laws in the regions where our users reside.


    2. Data Collection


      1. ​Minimal Data Collection


        Key Wallet operates as a decentralized wallet, meaning we do not hold custody of user assets or maintain a central server. Therefore, we collect minimal personal data from users, limited to situations where you voluntarily provide it. This includes:

        • Contacting our support team.

        • Participating in surveys, promotions, or other voluntary engagements.


          Examples of minimal data include IP addresses, device information, or other

          non-personal usage data for purposes such as error logging and service diagnostics.


          Definition of Personal Information: Personal information refers to any data that can be used to identify an individual, such as names, contact information, and identifiable numbers.

      2. ​Third-Party Integrations


        Certain third-party services integrated within Key Wallet, such as Matcha (for swaps) and on/off-ramp providers like MoonPay and Transak, may collect and use data as part of their operations. We recommend reviewing their privacy policies for details on

        their data collection practices and how they may handle your information. It is important to note that these third-party providers may collect personal and non-personal information independently from Key Wallet, and Key Wallet does not control how this data is processed.


    3. Data Usage


      We may use the information we collect for various purposes, including but not limited to:

      • Improving our application and overall user experience.

      • Logging errors and diagnostics to enhance service reliability.

      • Ensuring security and preventing fraud or unauthorized access.


      Any data collected for these purposes is anonymized or aggregated wherever possible, ensuring that it cannot be used to personally identify you.

      If services like Tangem cards or other third-party integrations collect data, please be aware that we do not control how those third parties handle your data. It’s essential to consult their respective privacy policies for more information.


    4. Data Sharing and Disclosure


      1. ​User Data


        Key Wallet does not share or sell user data to third parties for marketing or any other purposes. Our users’ privacy is a priority, and we uphold strict standards regarding data sharing.

      2. ​Third-Party Service Providers


        If data sharing with third-party service providers occurs, we will clarify how and why it happens. Such sharing may be necessary to provide services you request, and we will ensure that any data shared is minimized and compliant with relevant regulations. This may include sharing anonymized data with cloud service providers or analytics platforms to help us improve the user experience.

        We may also be required to disclose personal data if compelled by law enforcement or in response to a legal obligation, in accordance with applicable regulations.


    5. Security Measures


      We implement robust security measures to protect the information we collect from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. This includes:

      • Strong encryption protocols to safeguard sensitive information.

      • Secure key storage practices ensuring that user data is protected.

      • Security practices related to Tangem card integration to enhance user safety.

      • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) options for added account protection.

      • Regular security audits by independent third parties to verify the effectiveness of our security measures.

      We use encryption standards such as AES-256 to ensure data protection, and we review our security protocols regularly to adapt to new threats.

      However, please note that no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

      Additionally, users are responsible for maintaining the security of their own devices and private keys. If a private key is lost, Key Wallet cannot recover the associated assets.


    6. User Rights


      You have the following rights regarding your personal data:


      • Access: You can request access to the personal data we hold about you and receive a copy of that data.

      • Correction: You can request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.

      • Deletion: You can request the deletion of your personal data, subject to any legal obligations that may require us to retain it.

      • Restriction of Processing: You can request that we limit the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, such as where the accuracy is contested.

      For users in the European Union, you also have the right to data portability, allowing you to request that we transfer your data to another service provider in a structured, commonly used format.

      To exercise any of your rights, please contact us at [support@keywallet.com].


    7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies


      Currently, Key Wallet does not use cookies or tracking technologies to collect data about your use of the service. However, if we implement such technologies in the future for purposes like analytics, we will inform you about what data is tracked, how it is used, and provide options to opt out. Please note that third-party providers such as MoonPay or Transak may use cookies, and we recommend reviewing their privacy policies for further details.


    8. Data Retention


      We retain your information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy or as required by law. When we no longer need your information, we will securely delete or anonymize it in accordance with applicable laws.

      For diagnostic data and error logs, we retain the data for a maximum of 30 days unless longer retention is required by law. Voluntarily submitted data, such as customer support requests, may be retained for up to 12 months.


    9. International Users


      Key Wallet operates globally. As such, user data may be handled across different regions. Where necessary, we use Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) to ensure that your data is transferred internationally in compliance with applicable regulations. We strive to comply with major regulations, including GDPR for European users, ensuring that your data is handled according to applicable legal standards.


    10. Changes to the Privacy Policy


We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. Significant changes will be communicated to users before they take effect, ensuring transparency in how your information is handled. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. This revision clearly emphasizes Key Wallet’s decentralized nature while outlining third-party integrations and highlighting user responsibilities regarding security measures associated with asset management through tools like the Tangem card.
